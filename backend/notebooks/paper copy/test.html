Table of Contents

  1. Introduction

    This section introduces the concept of Free/Open Source Software (F/OSS) and its development process. It discusses the terminology used in the F/OSS community and the significance of knowledge sharing within these projects. The section also outlines the scope and objectives of the paper.

  2. Theoretical Foundation

    This section provides the theoretical background necessary to understand the dynamics of knowledge sharing in F/OSS projects. It introduces the Knowledge Sharing Model (KSM) and explains the processes of externalization and internalization of knowledge within mailing lists.

    1. Knowledge Sharing Model

      Describes the KSM and how it applies to mailing list interactions in F/OSS projects. It explains the roles of knowledge seekers and providers and the process of knowledge transfer within the community.

    2. Process of Measuring Knowledge

      Discusses the distinction between tacit and explicit knowledge and the challenges of measuring knowledge sharing activities. It introduces the metrics used to quantify knowledge sharing in F/OSS mailing lists.

  3. Relevant Prior Work and Research Questions

    Reviews existing literature on knowledge sharing in F/OSS projects and identifies gaps in current research. It formulates specific research questions that the paper aims to address.

  4. Methodology and Data

    Describes the research methodology, including data collection, extraction, and cleaning processes. It explains the sampling method and the criteria used to select the mailing lists for analysis.

    1. Sampling and Data Collection

      Details the selection of the Debian project and the specific mailing lists used in the study. It outlines the data collection period and the format of the archived email messages.

    2. Data Extraction

      Explains the coding schema and the Python script used to extract data from the mbox files. It describes how the extracted data was stored in a MySQL database for analysis.

    3. Data Cleaning

      Discusses the data cleaning process, including the removal of invalid messages and email addresses. It explains the criteria used to identify and remove irrelevant data.

    4. Identification of List Participants

      Describes the methods used to identify posters and repliers in the mailing lists. It explains how the data was processed to track individual participants and their contributions.

  5. Results and Discussions

    Presents the findings of the study, including descriptive statistics and analysis of posting and replying activities. It discusses the correlation between posts and replies and the implications for knowledge sharing in F/OSS projects.

    1. Posting and Replying Activities of Mailing Lists Participants

      Analyzes the distribution of posts and replies in the Developer and User mailing lists. It provides statistical measures and visualizations to illustrate the differences in participant activities.

    2. Relationship Between Participants\' Posting and Replying Activities

      Examines the correlation between the number of posts and replies made by participants. It uses regression analysis to explore the nature of this relationship.

    3. Knowledge Sharing in Self-Organizing Communities

      Discusses the self-organizing nature of F/OSS communities and how knowledge sharing activities follow a power-law distribution. It introduces the concept of "cubic fractal distribution" to explain the observed patterns.

  6. Summary

    Summarizes the key findings of the study and answers the research questions posed earlier. It highlights the main contributions of the paper to the understanding of knowledge sharing in F/OSS projects.

  7. Validity Threats

    Discusses potential threats to the validity of the study, including the limitations of the data and the generalizability of the findings. It addresses the impact of data cleaning on the results and the exclusion of certain participants.

  8. Future Work

    Outlines potential avenues for future research based on the findings of the study. It suggests further investigation into knowledge sharing patterns in other F/OSS projects and the use of additional data sources.

  9. Conclusion
